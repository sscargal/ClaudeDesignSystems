Can you help me understand how transformer attention works? I keep reading about it but the math isn't clicking.

2:14 PM
C

Happy to walk through it. Think of attention as a soft, learned lookup table — rather than fetching one specific value, you retrieve a weighted blend of all values based on how relevant each one is to your query.

The core operation is:

Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QKᵀ / √dₖ) · V

Three matrices — queries, keys, and values. Each token in your sequence produces one of each. The dot-product of a query with all keys gives you relevance scores; softmax turns those into probabilities; multiply by values to get the output.

What part would you like to dig into first — the intuition, the scaling factor, or multi-head attention?

2:14 PM
S

The scaling factor — why divide by √dₖ specifically?

2:16 PM
C

Good instinct to pull on that thread. When dₖ is large, dot products grow proportionally — their variance scales with dₖ. Without the scaling, the softmax input sits in a region where gradients are extremely small (the "saturated" regime), which slows or stalls learning.

Dividing by √dₖ keeps the dot products at roughly unit variance regardless of dimension size, which keeps the softmax outputs well-spread rather than collapsing to a one-hot distribution.

If you've worked with layer normalization, it's the same intuition — you're stabilizing the scale of activations to keep gradients healthy.
2:17 PM
S

That makes total sense. Now explain multi-head attention in one sentence.
